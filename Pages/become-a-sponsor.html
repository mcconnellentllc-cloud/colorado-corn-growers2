OUR PROVEN TRACK RECORD

Disaster Program Victories

American agriculture climbed out of a 7-year recession capped by COVID-19 uncertainty and trade war challenges. CCGA's advocacy work has secured well over $1.2 billion in critical assistance for Colorado farmers since 2018. Our wins include over $100 million from Market Facilitation Programs, over $400 million from CFAP 1 and 2, over $300 million in ARC and PLC payments, over $200 million in PPP loans, and over $500 million in crop insurance indemnities.

Current victories continue - CCGA's advocacy through SWCA partnerships helped secure ECAP (Emergency Commodity Assistance Program) which has delivered $8 billion nationally for 2024 economic losses. Most critically, SWCA's successful fight to include excessive heat as a qualifying peril in SDRP expanded Colorado's eligibility from just 24 drought-designated counties to statewide coverage - potentially multiplying Colorado's share of the $16 billion SDRP program.

Farm Bill Wins

Every Farm Bill cycle, CCGA fights for provisions that benefit Colorado corn growers. From crop insurance improvements to conservation program enhancements, our advocacy has brought over $1.2 billion in support to Colorado agriculture since 2018. Through our SWCA alliance, we secured critical disaster designations and successfully advocated for excessive heat inclusion in federal disaster programs.

CCGA continues mounting strong defenses against elimination of stepped-up basis tax policies that would disproportionately affect farm families, while actively promoting "climate smart agriculture" policies that recognize the unique realities of the western Great Plains. Our ongoing partnership with NCGA defends current market access and fights for expanding markets through ethanol and international trade deals.

Regulatory Relief

CCGA has successfully fought against burdensome labor regulations that threatened Colorado corn producers. When SB-021 proposed unjust labor regulations that would have devastated family farms, CCGA advocated aggressively alongside strong allies like Senator Cleave Simpson and Senator Rod Pelton to defend Colorado agriculture.

Senator Simpson, a Colorado rancher and farmer who serves as general manager of the Rio Grande Water Conservation District, understands that agriculture faces unique challenges. Senator Byron Pelton has sponsored legislation to repeal certain provisions that prohibit employers from interfering with agricultural employees' access to service providers. Our regulatory advocacy has saved Colorado corn growers millions in compliance costs while protecting the viability of family farming operations across the state.

Taking on the Corn Belt

We've challenged the Corn Belt's dominance to ensure Colorado gets equitable opportunities to grow corn. Our advocacy has secured fair treatment in crop insurance, conservation programs, and research funding - ensuring Western agriculture isn't left behind. While other regions had automatic advantages, CCGA fought to level the playing field for Colorado corn growers.

Critical SWCA Victory: Excessive Heat Qualification

Without SWCA's successful advocacy to include excessive heat as a qualifying peril in SDRP, Colorado would have been limited to just 24 drought-designated counties for disaster payments. This single advocacy victory potentially multiplied Colorado's SDRP payments by several times over what drought-only qualification would have provided, bringing millions more in disaster relief to Colorado corn producers statewide.
