ABOUT CCGA

Volunteer Corn Producers Fighting for Colorado Agriculture

We Are Real Farmers, Not Bureaucrats

The Growers Association of Colorado Corn is comprised of volunteer corn producers with a deep passion for farm policy. We don't just manage programs - we fight for the policies that keep Colorado agriculture viable.

Our Grassroots Approach

Unlike bureaucratic organizations that operate on automatic funding streams, CCGA is driven by volunteer corn growers who roll up their sleeves and get to work. We're not paid staff managing existing programs - we're working farmers who understand what it takes to survive in today's agricultural economy.

Every Friday Morning: The Fight Continues

Most every Friday morning, you'll find CCGA volunteer board members on phone calls and video conferences with lobbyists, politicians, and policy makers. Why? Because Washington never stops, and it's a constant battle to keep Colorado corn growers at the forefront of funding and policy decisions. While others wait for things to happen, we make them happen.

This isn't a hobby for us - it's survival. When disaster strikes, when trade wars threaten our markets, when regulations threaten our operations, we're already on the phone, already working the relationships, already fighting for Colorado corn growers.

Our Proven Track Record

Since 2018, our volunteer advocacy efforts have secured well over $1.2 billion in critical assistance for Colorado agriculture. This didn't happen by accident - it happened because CCGA volunteers were in Washington, on the phone, and at the table when these programs were being developed.

100% Independent, Voluntary Support

We collect NO automatic funding streams. We receive NO government mandated fees. We depend entirely on voluntary memberships and sponsorships from those who believe in effective advocacy.

This independence means we can fight for what's right, not what's politically convenient. We answer to corn growers, not government bureaucrats.

Strategic Partnerships That Multiply Our Impact

CCGA doesn't fight alone. Our volunteer board members have built strategic alliances that amplify Colorado's voice on the national stage.

Southwest Council of Agribusiness (SWCA)

Our partnership with SWCA gives Colorado corn growers a powerful voice in Washington. Together, we've secured hundreds of millions in disaster relief and critical policy victories like the excessive heat SDRP qualification that dramatically expanded Colorado's eligibility for disaster payments.

National Corn Growers Association (NCGA)

Through NCGA, our volunteer advocacy reaches national policy makers and ensures Colorado's voice is heard in every major agricultural decision in Washington, D.C. This partnership helps us defend current markets and fight for new opportunities like ethanol expansion and international trade deals.

The Burlington Connection

Our Burlington office serves as the hub of our volunteer operation. Located in the heart of Colorado corn country, this office represents our grassroots commitment to staying connected to the farmers we serve.

From Burlington, our volunteer board members coordinate their Friday morning advocacy sessions, maintain relationships with key political allies, and ensure Colorado corn growers have a voice in policy decisions being made hundreds of miles away in Washington.

This isn't a corporate headquarters - it's a working office where real farmers gather to fight for real solutions to the challenges facing Colorado agriculture.
